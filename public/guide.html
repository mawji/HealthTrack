How to use your app

Welcome to HealthTrack

Your body, beautifully measured. HealthTrack pulls your steps, heart, sleep, workouts and labs into one calm dashboard, and gives you an AI coach who knows your numbers. This guide walks you through it — starting with the few settings that switch everything on, then every screen, and finally the quiet work it does in the background.

Everything stays on your machine. Your health data is stored locally and never leaves your device — the only thing that ever goes out is the message you send to the AI model you choose. Screens in this guide use sample data.

Start here — five minutes to a working app

Open Settings (bottom of the left menu, or the ⚙ on mobile). Three things turn the app on. Do them in order and you'll get value from your very first day.

  1. Connect Google Health. This is what fills the app with your real steps, heart rate, sleep, workouts and body measurements. It's a one-time sign-in — see below.
  2. Choose the model that powers your coach. Connect a ChatGPT subscription, paste an API key for any provider, or run a fully private local model.
  3. Fill in your profile. Sex, height, weight and goals let the app personalise your calorie and macro targets and let the coach tailor its advice.

1 · Settings → Integrations → Connect Google Health

1 2 3
  1. 1The eight Settings tabs. Everything you can configure lives here — we cover each one below.
  2. 2Connection status. Shows whether HealthTrack is linked to Google Health yet.
  3. 3Connect Google Health. Tap, sign in once, and your dashboard fills in. Until you do, the app runs on demo data.

2 · Settings → AI Assistant → pick your coach's brain

1 2 3 4
  1. 1Primary model. The model used for every coach reply and for reading your meal photos. A capable cloud model gives the best coaching.
  2. 2Secondary (fallback). Used automatically if the primary is unavailable — handy belt-and-braces.
  3. 3ChatGPT (Subscription). Sign in with your existing ChatGPT Plus/Pro account using a one-time code — no API key, no extra bill.
  4. 4Ollama (Local). Run a model entirely on your own hardware for total privacy. Other options: an OpenAI / Gemini / Anthropic / OpenRouter API key.
One thing to know: reading food photos needs a vision-capable model. If you pick a text-only local model as your primary, set a vision-capable secondary so photo logging still works.

3 · Settings → Profile → tell the app about you

Set your biological sex, date of birth, height, current weight, activity level and goal (lose / maintain / gain, with a weekly rate). The app turns these into daily calorie and macronutrient targets you'll see on Daily, Food and Trends. The optional conditions field (e.g. diabetes, high cholesterol) lets the coach keep its advice relevant and conservative.

That's the essential setup. The remaining tabs — Intelligence, Telegram, Sharing, Preferences and Data & Storage — are all optional and explained next.

Every setting, explained

Settings has eight tabs. You've met the first three; here's what the rest do.

Profile · Integrations · AI Assistant

Covered in Start here above: your body details and targets, the Google Health connection, and the model that powers your coach.

Intelligence — the coach's background brain

When this is on, once a night the coach quietly reviews your week and month — activity, sleep, habits, food and labs — and writes a Wellbeing reflection (you'll find it under Journal). The basic review is deterministic and free. If you want it to also distil durable memories about you, point it at a cheap or local model:

  • Run at (quiet hour) — the local time the nightly review happens.
  • How readily it forms memories — conservative keeps only durable facts; eager also notes emerging tendencies.
  • Tier-1 / Tier-2 models — Tier-1 is a cheap/local first pass; Tier-2 (optional) refines it. Use a small model here, separate from your main coach.
  • Background activity — a full, plain-English log of every run, what it did and a rough token estimate. Nothing is a black box.

Telegram — coach + nudges in your pocket

Pair a Telegram bot and you can chat with the same coach, log workouts, food and water, and pull your metrics — all from Telegram. Message @BotFather in Telegram, run /newbot, and paste the token it gives you. Below that, Proactive nudges (opt-in) sends gentle reminders when you're falling behind on your goals or habits, plus a short daily report.

Privacy note: Telegram messages are not end-to-end encrypted — don't send anything there you wouldn't put in a normal chat. The bot only answers people you've paired.

Sharing — let a partner or family member in

Sharing lets specific, approved people reach your coach through the same Telegram bot — useful for a partner, parent or anyone helping look after your health. You choose exactly who, and what they can see. (Set up Telegram first, since sharing uses the same bot.)

Preferences — the small comforts

  • Theme mode — light or dark.
  • Auto refresh — how often the app quietly re-syncs your data in the background (e.g. every 5 minutes).
  • Timezone — the zone your health data is mapped into, so days line up correctly.
  • Medication supply tracking — count pills down as you take them and remind you to refill when you're low.

Data & Storage — your history, your machine

Days older than three days are cached in a local database (archive.db) so your Trends and Records load instantly without slow round-trips to Google. Use Synchronize 1-Year History once to back-fill a full year. The bottom line — restated here in black and white — is that your data never leaves this machine except for the calls to the AI provider you chose.

What happens in the background

A lot of HealthTrack's value is quiet. Here's the work it does without being asked.

Your readiness score

Google doesn't provide a readiness number, so HealthTrack derives one. Each morning it compares last night's heart-rate variability, resting heart rate and sleep against your own rolling two-week baseline. When they're better than usual you score high; when they're worse, it dips — a nudge to take it easier. It's tuned to be read as a trend, not an exact figure, and it's built on the same physiology that WHOOP and Oura use.

Cardio load

Also not in the Google data, so it's computed in-app from how hard and how long your heart works during activity (heart rate relative to your resting rate, weighted toward recent days). Watching the trend — and the balance between this week and your recent average — helps you build fitness steadily without spiking into overtraining.

The nightly reflection & growing memory

If you switch on Intelligence, once a night the coach reviews your week and month and writes the Wellbeing reflection you read in the Journal — a five-domain audit plus a short narrative and a few suggested actions. With a model attached it can also distil durable memories about you (under guard-rails you control), which then show up on the Memory page labelled "noticed in review". Every run is logged in plain English in Settings → Intelligence.

Proactive nudges & reminders

With Telegram paired and nudges enabled, the coach reaches out when you're drifting from your goals or habits, and can send a short daily report. You can also ask it to remind you of anything — "remind me to take my evening walk at 6" — and it will, both in-app and on Telegram. Reminders you set this way are one-offs that fire exactly when asked.

The coach is also your logger

You rarely need a form. Telling the coach "logged a 30-minute walk", "two glasses of water" or "had oatmeal for breakfast" records it the same as if you'd filled one in — see below.

Tidying your workouts & caching your history

When a watch and phone both record the same session, HealthTrack merges the overlapping entries into one so your day isn't double-counted. And, as noted under Data & Storage, older days are cached locally so Trends and Records open instantly.

Three ways to log anything

However you prefer to work, recording something is quick:

  • Food screen photograph a meal, scan a barcode, search, or describe it in words.
  • + Log button the floating button (bottom-right on every screen) is a quick menu for activity, hydration, weight, blood pressure, glucose and more — tap, type a number, done.
  • Tell your coach type or speak it in plain language and the coach records it for you.

Install it on your phone & computer

HealthTrack installs like a normal app — no app store needed:

Phone: open your HealthTrack address in the browser. On Android (Chrome), tap the menu → Install app. On iPhone (Safari), tap Share → Add to Home Screen.

Computer: open it in Chrome or Edge and click the install icon in the address bar.

It then opens full-screen with its own icon and stays signed in.

If a screen won't load away from home: make sure the Tailscale app is running and signed in on that device — it's what privately and securely connects you to your HealthTrack. Your setup contact can help if you're unsure.
